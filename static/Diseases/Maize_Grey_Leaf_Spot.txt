Grey leaf spot (GLS) is a foliar fungal disease that affects maize, also known as corn. GLS is considered one of the most significant yield-limiting diseases of corn worldwide. It is caused by the fungus Cercospora zeae-maydis. The disease is now recognised as one of the most significant yield-limiting diseases of maize worldwide.

Symptoms and Signs

Symptoms of gray leaf spot are usually first noticed in the lower leaves. Initially, lesions of gray leaf spot begin as a small dot with a yellow halo. Lesions will elongate over time running parallel to the veins becoming pale brown to gray and rectangular in shape with blunt ends.
